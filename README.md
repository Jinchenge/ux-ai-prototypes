# UX AI Prototypes

UX AI Prototypes is a public design project exploring responsible AI-assisted workflows for UX designers and product teams.

This repository documents templates, prompts, prototype concepts, and practical experiments for using AI APIs in product design work. The focus is not only on generating ideas faster, but also on keeping the design process clear, responsible, and user-centered.

## About Me

I am Junxin Xiao, a UX designer interested in how AI can support research, interaction design, usability testing, content design, and product decision-making.

As the primary maintainer of this project, I use this repository to organize public examples and reusable UX materials that other designers and product builders can learn from.

## Project Goals

- Explore how AI APIs can support real UX design workflows.
- Create reusable templates for research synthesis, personas, journey maps, and usability testing.
- Document responsible AI design patterns for designers and product teams.
- Prototype AI-assisted workflows for content suggestions, design critique, and research analysis.
- Share practical examples that are understandable for both designers and developers.

## Planned Use Cases

### Research Synthesis

Use AI to help summarize interview notes, identify recurring user needs, and organize findings into themes while keeping the original research context visible.

### Persona and Journey Mapping

Experiment with AI-assisted persona drafts, journey map structure, and opportunity areas based on research inputs.

### Usability Test Analysis

Use AI to help cluster usability issues, generate severity notes, and prepare clearer reports for product teams.

### UX Writing and Microcopy

Prototype workflows for generating and comparing product copy, onboarding text, empty states, error messages, and accessibility-friendly language.

### Design Critique Assistant

Explore how AI can provide structured feedback on product screens, flows, and interaction patterns from a UX perspective.

## Responsible AI Principles

This project follows a few basic principles:

- AI should support design judgment, not replace it.
- User privacy and sensitive data must be protected.
- AI-generated outputs should be reviewed before being used in product decisions.
- Research findings should stay connected to real user evidence.
- Designers should make AI usage transparent when it affects users or teams.
